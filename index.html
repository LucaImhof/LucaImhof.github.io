Handyman - Hard work, dedication, and achievement are how I would describe my time working alongside a handyman. From waking up at 5 am and not being home until 5 pm were some of the worst and best days of my life. Having to quickly pick up skills ranging from plumbing to electrical work and even gardening, I was able to bring joy to happy customers and have a good sense of achievement after a job well done. Doing this line of work for 3 years allowed me to value the quality of work that I had done. I learned quickly that simple situations do not always have simple answers. It taught me to work hard towards an end product and to think outside the box in tricky situations.

Boba Barista - Working in the boba industry opened me to a whole new line of work that I was not used to. In this field, Customer service played a big roll. It was an experience that I am glad to have had at an early stage of my life. In such a hectic environment, patience was what allowed me to succeed in making sure everything could stay under control. This job had a certain flow that could easily be torn apart by one mistake, and being patient when situations became out of hand made the difference between being yelled at by multiple people or customers walking away satisfied. In such a fast pace environment, it was important to learn quickly and be able to deescalate tension. Communicating between coworkers and customers made it so the work flow could remain stable.

Volunteer Experience - During high school, I joined a club that focused on a lot of community service and volunteer programs. There I learned a deeper understanding of others and the importance of showing empathy and compassion towards others as well. I worked with the Kiwanis Family House in organizing donations for families as well as volunteering with others in cleaning the living spaces.
